Thinking about how I view graphic design as a whole is something that is very challenging. There are so many things that graphic design could be! In this piece, I explain what the true meaning of design is to me.

OTHER ANGLES

MANIFESTO

As a junior majoring in graphic design, I believe design and designers are underrated, powerful, an art that rules the world. Many people who are not involved in the world of design realize the worth of design and the designer behind it all. Designers create amazing and strong advertisements and pieces that convey specific things. It is a trade that not everyone can acquire, yet it so needed to make the economy go round. Without design, the world would be a very distant and angry place.

Design is underrated. Too often do people think being the friend of a designer is a benefit for free work. It is not okay to be a "friend" who neglects to see the hard work and effort that goes into a simple piece. The wages of designers for major companies is pitiful. Designers are trained in many software's, devices and life experiences that allow them to create the best design for the task. Often times, the time that goes into one project is more tedious and demanding than that of engineers, yet they come out of college making great money. Design is simply put, underrated and underappreciated.

Design is powerful. A tool that can create controversy or unity in a matter of seconds looking at it. Those who are not designers fail to realize the importance that design has in the entire world. Everywhere you look, a designer has deliberately sought out a way to make something useful—from being easy to read to being interesting to look at—design has the influence to manipulate the human brain without even asking for permission. This kind of power is stronger than that of the president of the United States and we don't even realize it.

Design is art. No, design is not a fine art like painting, but an art as in a trade that not everyone can do. A designer must have knowledge of fine arts to incorporate those techniques into their work. A designer must practice their trade and learn from their mistakes. It is not something that comes easy. So while designing might not be fine art, it is an art in the sense that it is something that is desirable yet not everyone can create it themselves.

As designers, we need to stay true to ourselves. It is vital in the end result. Using our skills to create powerful messages is part of our art form. We must push ideas further to becoming appreciated, to become noticed. Designers are always the one behind closed doors, that no one sees or thinks about. Standing up for ourselves, our designs and our art is the only way that we will be able to make a difference in the world.
